LI-GS: Geometrically Accurate Reconstruction Using Gaussian Splatting with LiDAR Incorporated

Zhejiang University
*Corresponding author

Abstract

Large-scale 3D reconstruction is critical in the field of robotics, and the effectiveness of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) for achieving accurate reconstruction has been demonstrated. However, ensuring geometric accuracy in outdoor and unbounded scenes remains a significant challenge. This study introduces LI-GS, a reconstruction system that leverages the integration of LiDAR and Gaussian Splatting to enhance geometric accuracy in large-scale and unbounded scenes. We employ 2D Gaussain surfels as our map representation to enhance surface alignment. Additionally, a novel and efficient modeling method is proposed to convert LiDAR point clouds to plane-constrained multimodal Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs). The GMMs are utilized during both initialization and optimization stages to establish accurate and continuous geometric constraints. This approach ensures sufficient supervision over the entire scene while mitigating the risk of over-fitting. Furthermore, GMMs are utilized during mesh extraction to eliminate artifacts and improve the overall geometric quality. Experiments demonstrate that our method outperforms state-of-the-art methods in surface reconstruction, achieving significantly higher accuracy compared to both LiDAR-based methods (with an improvement of 52.6%) and Gaussian-based methods (with an improvement of 68.7%).

The overview of our system. Our system involves three steps: (a) We utilize the plane-constrained incremental 4D GMM modeling method to generate initial Gaussian surfels. (b) To optimize the Gaussian surfels, our system applies global image normalization, local GMM normalization, and geometry-aware density control. (c) We eliminate erroneous samples using a coarse-to-fine method, and then apply the screened Poisson reconstruction method to extract meshes.
